        • A: These summary statistics apply only under certain assumptions and are misleading.       • I love box plots. When are box plots most useful?
        • A: They combine lot of information in single graph. Box plots allow us to see if distribution is symmetric, how the weight is distributed between central peak and tails and outliers are not dropped. Most useful are when comparing several distributions against each other.
      • The book doesn't mention violin plots. Are those better or worse than box plots? Why?
        • A: They are more informative than a plain box plot. They are useful when data have more than one peak (multimodal distribution). In this case a violin plot clearly shows the presence of different peaks, their position and relative amplitude. Disadvantage of violin plots is that they are less popular, their meaning can be harder to grasp for many readers not familiar with the violin plot representation.
